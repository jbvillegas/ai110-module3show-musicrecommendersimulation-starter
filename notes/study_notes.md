# Study Notes for Music Recommendation and AI Workflows

Music recommendation systems often combine user preferences like genre, mood, and energy with song features such as tempo, popularity, and acousticness.

When a user likes acoustic music, the system can reward higher acousticness scores. When a user likes high energy, the recommender should prefer songs with energy values closer to the target.

Agentic AI workflows use a loop of plan, act, and check. The agent decides what it will do, performs the action, and then validates the result. This improves reliability in code generation and question writing.

Retrieval-augmented generation means the model first looks up facts in a document collection before answering. A study bot can search notes, retrieve the best sections, and then generate a quiz question grounded in that information.

A specialized model or fine-tuned model is one that is adapted for a task, like writing in a brand voice or producing study questions instead of general chat responses.

Reliable AI systems also measure consistency. A simple check compares repeated outputs over multiple runs and flags differences if the answers drift.
